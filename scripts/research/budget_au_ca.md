## Part 4: Key Observations

### Australia vs. Canada Comparison

1. **Tuition ranges are broadly similar** at top universities: AUD $38,000--$60,600 vs. CAD $30,000--$70,894
2. **Engineering** is consistently among the most expensive fields in both countries
3. **Humanities/Arts** is the most affordable discipline in both countries
4. **Living costs** are generally comparable, though Montreal and Alberta cities are notably cheaper
5. **Health insurance:** Australia's OSHC (~AUD $600/yr) is simpler; Canada varies wildly by province ($0 in Alberta to ~$1,000 in Quebec)
6. **Visa costs:** Australia's student visa (AUD $2,000) is dramatically more expensive than Canada's study permit (CAD $150)
7. **Program duration:** Australia UG is typically 3 years (saving a year vs. Canada's 4 years), which can offset higher annual tuition

### Most Affordable Options (by total annual cost)

**Australia:**
- University of Adelaide (Arts): ~AUD $58,000--$62,000/yr total
- UWA (Arts): ~AUD $58,000--$62,000/yr total

**Canada:**
- Universite de Montreal: ~CAD $42,000--$48,000/yr total (French instruction)
- University of Calgary: ~CAD $44,000--$48,000/yr total (English, free health coverage)
- University of Alberta: ~CAD $47,000--$51,000/yr total (English, free health coverage)

### Most Expensive Options (by total annual cost)

**Australia:**
- University of Sydney (Engineering): ~AUD $86,000--$94,000/yr total
- UNSW (Science/Engineering): ~AUD $83,000--$90,000/yr total

**Canada:**
- University of Waterloo (Engineering): ~CAD $85,000--$90,000/yr total
- University of Toronto (Engineering): ~CAD $86,000--$93,000/yr total
