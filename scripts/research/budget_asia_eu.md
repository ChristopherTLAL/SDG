## 3. Japan

**Currency:** JPY (Japanese Yen)
**Academic year:** April – March (some programs start October)
**Program duration:** Master's 2 years; undergraduate 4 years

### Key Feature: Standardized National University Tuition

Japanese national universities have a **standardized fee** set by MEXT (Ministry of Education):
- **Enrollment fee (入学金):** 282,000 JPY (one-time)
- **Annual tuition:** 535,800 JPY (~same for ALL disciplines)

This is remarkably affordable. Private universities charge significantly more.

### 5.2 Germany

**Currency:** EUR (Euro)
**Key Feature: ZERO TUITION at public universities (except Baden-Wurttemberg)**

Germany is one of the most attractive study destinations because most public universities charge **no tuition fees** for international students. Only **Baden-Wurttemberg** (Heidelberg, etc.) reintroduced tuition for non-EU students in 2017.

#### Tuition (Annual, EUR)

| University | 理科 Science | 工科 Engineering | 文社科 Humanities & SS | 商科 Business |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TU Munich | ~260 (Semesterbeitrag only) | ~260 | ~260 | ~260 |
| LMU Munich | ~260 | N/A | ~260 | ~260 |
| Heidelberg University | 3,000 (non-EU fee) + ~340 | 3,000 + ~340 | 3,000 + ~340 | 3,000 + ~340 |
| Humboldt University Berlin | ~320 (includes transit pass) | ~320 | ~320 | ~320 |
| Freie Universität Berlin | ~320 (includes transit pass) | ~320 | ~320 | ~320 |
| TU Berlin | ~320 | ~320 | ~320 | ~320 |

> **Semesterbeitrag:** The ~130–170 EUR/semester "semester contribution" covers student services, a public transit pass (Semesterticket), and student union fees. This is NOT tuition.
>
> **Baden-Wurttemberg surcharge:** Universities in this state (Heidelberg, KIT, Stuttgart, Freiburg, etc.) charge non-EU students ~1,500 EUR/semester (3,000 EUR/year).
>
> **TU Munich note:** As of 2024/2025, TU Munich introduced tuition fees for new non-EU students in some programs (~2,000-4,000 EUR/semester). Check current status.

### Important Caveats
1. **Tuition fees change annually** — always verify on the university's official website before making decisions
2. **Exchange rates fluctuate** — the CNY/USD equivalents are approximate guides only
3. **Scholarships can dramatically reduce costs** — many universities offer merit and need-based scholarships specifically for international students
4. **Living cost estimates assume a modest student lifestyle** — actual costs depend heavily on personal choices
5. **MBA/business programs** are significantly more expensive than other disciplines almost everywhere
6. **Program duration matters for total cost**: European master's programs are often 1–2 years vs. US 2-year norms
7. **Working rights vary**: Most student visas allow limited part-time work (10–20 hrs/week during term), which can offset living costs
8. **TU Munich tuition update**: TU Munich began introducing tuition for non-EU international students starting 2024/2025 academic year for new enrollments. Amounts vary by program (reportedly EUR 2,000–4,000/semester for some programs). This is a significant change from the previous zero-tuition model. Verify current status.
